    Astral Projection

    Topic

    Astral Projection

    Lecturer

    David Turlington

    Date

    23/08/2017

    Place

    Room 2

    Description:

    David Turlington explained difficult subject in studying Buddhism, which is the various states of consciousness. He talked about practices such as meditation and introspection that allows for the believer to achieve enlightenment through self-discovery. One aspect that is rarely discussed and on which he focused is the levels of consciousness and how these too can be honed and manipulated.

    David exposed that most of a person’s memories are founded on the realm of reality or consciousness. It occupies most of a person’s thoughts, with people being dependent on their senses. Nonetheless, there are other planes of consciousness to consider. When one dreams, for example, they are between both the spiritual and physical worlds. Most people do not deliberately moderate their dreams.

    Lecturer introduced a practice called astral projection where in the conscious mind leaves the physical body intentionally. The exercise keeps the person attached to their physical body by a mental "umbilical cord" and allows them to travel within the astral plane. This is also known as an out-of-body experience.
